\subsection{SSM\ model with two lags}

Kurz's (2018) SSM has the following general from:\bsq\label{SSM}%
\begin{align}
\mathsf{Measurement}& :\quad Z_{t}=D_{1}X_{t}+D_{2}X_{t-1}+R\varepsilon _{t}
\label{ssm1} \\
\mathsf{State}& :\quad X_{t}=AX_{t-1}+C\varepsilon _{t},  \label{ssm2}
\end{align}%
\esq where $\varepsilon _{t}\sim MN(0,I_{m})$, $D_{1},D_{2},A,R$ are $C$ are
conformable system matrices, $Z_{t}$ the observed variable and $X_{t}$ the
latent state variable.

\subsection{Modified (standard) SSM}

The simple model takes the form:\bsq\label{clark0}%
\begin{align}
y_{t}& =g_{t-1}+\varepsilon _{1t} \\
g_{t}& =g_{t-1}+\varepsilon _{2t}
\end{align}%
\esq where the shocks $\left\{ \varepsilon _{it}\right\} _{i=1}^{2}$ are
assumed to be $i.i.d.$ standard normal and mutually uncorrelated, with unit
standard deviation. The modified (standard)\ SSM\ with shocks down the
bottom of state vector:%
\begin{align}

\subsection{Shock recovery SSM}

\begin{equation}
